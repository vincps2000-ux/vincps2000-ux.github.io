First Solution: 
select name as "Employee" from Employee As testedEmployee
where testedEmployee.salary > (SELECT max(Employee.salary) FROM Employee where testedEmployee.managerId = Employee.id 

Simple Subquery for every entry. Obviously slow.

Better:
inner join can be used on two versions of the same Table: 
from Employee e
inner join Employee m
on e.managerId = m.id 

This means instead of Table-size number of Database operations, only 1 is needed.
